Developer Rules

The Gitogether Code of Conduct

Mission: Gitogether is designed to connect serious developers to build real-world projects. To maintain the quality of our ecosystem, all users must adhere to these four core directives. Violations are monitored by platform admins and will result in account suspension.

01

Absolute Professionalism

Treat every interaction like a professional workplace. Harassment, toxicity, discrimination, or abusive language in project chats, code reviews, or peer ratings is strictly prohibited. Disagreements over architecture or code are fine; personal attacks are not.

02

Commitment & Integrity

Do not request to join a project if you do not have the bandwidth to contribute. "Ghosting" your team after being accepted halts development and ruins the experience for others. If you must step down from a project, communicate it clearly to the project owner.

03

Authentic Representation

Your developer profile must reflect your actual skill set. Do not list frameworks or languages you do not know just to get accepted into higher-tier projects. Falsifying your technical abilities wastes the project owner's time.

04

Constructive Feedback

Peer ratings are designed to build reputation, not to tear people down. All reviews must be honest, objective, and based purely on the developer's technical contribution and teamwork. Retaliatory or "review-bombing" ratings will be purged by platform admins.
